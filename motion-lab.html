SCROLL STAGGER
Internal · not shipped · 2026

Stagger that waits for the scroll.

Right now below-fold elements fire as soon as they peek past the bottom edge, then run a 1.56s settle. So they start, and often finish, while still parked at the foot of the screen, before you've scrolled them into view. The motion outruns the reader.

Three ways to fix it, roughest to safest, all on the existing tokens. Scroll each block: the specimen sits well below the fold so you feel the trigger point, not just see it.

Recommendation: A (scroll-driven) for the homepage, B for everything else. A ties motion to scroll position so it can't outrun the reader, costs zero JS, and degrades cleanly where unsupported. B is the same fix inside the observer we already ship, supported everywhere today. Both replace the .d1–.d4 delay ladder with a single --stagger index. C is the brief's on-load pattern, kept for above-fold heroes where there's no scroll to wait on.

A · recommended

Scroll-driven, view-timeline.

Progress is bound to the element's travel through the viewport via animation-timeline: view(). It resolves in the reading band, and scrolling back up plays it in reverse. No JS. --stagger offsets each item's range for the cascade.

Porting to the real motion system. A and B both retire the .d1–.d4 classes for one --stagger index set per child, which scales past 4 items for free.
