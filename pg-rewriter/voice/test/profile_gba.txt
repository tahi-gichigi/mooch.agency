"Hack" means two opposite things. It can mean an ugly, clumsy solution, the kind you're a little ashamed of. It can also mean a clever, ingenious one, the kind you're proud of. So how can the same word mean both? At first the two senses look like opposites. But look closer and they're more closely related than they seem. The ugly fix and the clever trick are often the same move, just judged from different angles. The contradiction dissolves once you see that.
