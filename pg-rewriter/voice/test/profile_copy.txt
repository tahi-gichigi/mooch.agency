The best way to get good at creative work isn't the way people usually tell you. You're told to copy what's already respected, the things that carry some cultural weight. But that's backwards. The better move is to copy what you actually like, whatever its status. When you imitate the things you're supposed to admire, the imitation is hollow. You're going through the motions. When you imitate what you genuinely love, something real happens. You engage with the work instead of performing engagement with it. Copy what you love, not what you're told to.
