Most people learning creative work are told to study the canon. Read the classics. Imitate what's been validated. It sounds right, but it's probably backwards.

The better approach is to copy what you actually love. Not what you think you should love—what you genuinely do. There's a difference, and it matters more than it looks.

When you imitate something prestigious that doesn't move you, the imitation stays hollow. You can reproduce the surface, but you can't reproduce what made it work, because you don't feel it. When you imitate something you love, you're reaching for the thing that got to you, and that reaching is where real skill develops.

Your taste is a clue about where your abilities want to go. Ignore it and you're working blind.
