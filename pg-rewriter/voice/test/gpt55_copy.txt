If you want to get good at creative work, don't start by copying the things you're supposed to admire. Copy the things you actually like.

This sounds wrong at first, because the admired things have a kind of official glow. They've been blessed by schools, critics, or time. So it seems safe to use them as models. But safe models often produce dead copies. You're trying to make something that looks like admiration, not something you can feel.

Your own taste is a better guide. It may be crude at first. It may point to things with no prestige at all. But if you really like something, you can see it from the inside. You notice what it's doing. You care enough to take it apart.

That matters. Imitating what you think you should like teaches you to fake. Imitating what you do like gives you a live wire to follow.
