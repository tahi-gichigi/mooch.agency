It must be acknowledged at the outset that there exists a puzzle of a rather considerable and arguably enduring nature. On the one hand, it is broadly understood that human beings are, in a fundamental sense, constituted of matter, and that this matter is, to all appearances, subject to and governed by the various physical laws that obtain throughout the natural world. On the other hand, it would seem to be the case that we nonetheless experience a pervasive and persistent sense that we are, in some meaningful respect, at liberty to determine our own actions through the exercise of choice. The question that consequently arises, and which has been the subject of no small measure of deliberation, concerns the manner in which these two ostensibly incompatible propositions might, in some fashion, be reconciled with one another.
