There's an old puzzle here, and it won't go away.

We're made of matter. And matter obeys physical laws. Drop a rock and it falls; the rock doesn't get a say. We're built out of the same stuff, so it would seem we don't get a say either.

But we feel like we do. All day long we feel as if we're choosing. You decide to get up, to stay, to read this sentence or stop. The sense of choosing isn't a faint one. It's everywhere, and it doesn't go away no matter how much you know about physics.

So which is it? If we're just matter running on physical laws, the choosing must be an illusion. And if the choosing is real, something has to give in the physics. The two stories don't fit together, and people have argued about how to make them fit for a very long time.
