Since I was about nine I've been puzzled by what seems like a contradiction: I'm made of matter that behaves predictably, yet I feel like I can choose what I want. At the time I was almost always in some kind of trouble, and I remember thinking this might offer a way out — maybe I wasn't responsible for my actions. I gave up on that, but the puzzle stayed.

The best way to explain the answer is to start with a version that's slightly wrong and then fix it. The wrong version: you can do what you want, but you can't want what you want. You control what you do, but you'll do what you want, and you can't control that.

The problem is that people do sometimes change what they want. Drug addicts do quit. People who want to like classical music or broccoli sometimes manage it. So we have to revise: you can do what you want, but you can't want to want what you want.

That's not quite right either, because you can change what you want to want. But we're getting closer. It's rare for anyone to change what they want to want, and the more iterations of "want to" you add, the rarer it gets.
