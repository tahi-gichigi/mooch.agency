It is worth noting, by way of preliminary observation, that the term "hack" is one which, somewhat curiously, has come to encompass two meanings that would appear, at least superficially, to be diametrically opposed to one another. On the one hand, the word is frequently utilised to denote a solution of a clumsy, inelegant, or otherwise aesthetically deficient character; on the other hand, it is also routinely deployed to characterise a manoeuvre of considerable ingenuity and cleverness. While these two interpretations might initially be perceived as standing in fundamental opposition, it could be argued that, upon closer and more sustained examination, the relationship between them is, in many respects, substantially more intimate and interconnected than a cursory consideration would tend to suggest. To a certain extent, the apparent contradiction may, arguably, dissolve under scrutiny.
