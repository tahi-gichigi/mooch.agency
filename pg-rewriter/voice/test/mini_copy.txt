If you want to get good at creative work, don't copy the things everyone says you should. Most advice tells you to study the respected stuff—the things on syllabuses and in museums. Of course that's not useless. Those works show you what's possible. But they also invite a hollow kind of imitation: you learn the manner without the hunger behind it. If instead you copy what you truly love, two things happen. You're willing to look closer, and you notice the odd, small moves that make it work. And you're more likely to follow them where they lead, not just paste them on the surface. Copying what you love doesn't make you derivative. It gives you material you care about, and that's where original work begins.
