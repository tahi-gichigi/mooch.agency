If you want to get good at any kind of creative work, the usual advice is wrong.

You're told to copy the great works, the ones with prizes and reputations attached. Study the canon. Imitate what the experts admire.

But there's a better way: copy what you actually like.

The trouble with copying what you're supposed to admire is that the admiration isn't yours. You're working from someone else's taste, and it shows. The imitation comes out hollow, because there's no real feeling behind it. You're going through the motions of liking something.

What you genuinely love is different. You don't have to fake your way into caring about it. You already see what's good in it, even if you can't say why, and that's exactly the thing worth chasing. The work you copy out of real enthusiasm pulls you somewhere. The work you copy out of duty just sits there.

So don't ask what you ought to admire. Ask what you can't stop looking at.
